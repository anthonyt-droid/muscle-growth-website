Phase One

The Training Stimulus

Muscle growth begins with a disruption of homeostasis. When you subject a muscle to a load it is not accustomed to, the body perceives this as a threat to its survival.

  • ✔ Resistance must be high enough to challenge the tissue.
  • ✔ The stimulus must be progressive (overload).

The Key Driver

Mechanical Tension

Mechanical tension is widely considered the most important factor for hypertrophy. It occurs when muscle fibers are stretched under load.

Passive Tension

Tension from stretching the muscle (long muscle lengths).

Active Tension

Tension created by the contraction of muscle fibers themselves.

Motor Unit Recruitment

To grow, we must recruit the High-Threshold Motor Units. These are the fibers with the greatest potential for growth.

Type I (Slow Twitch)

Recruited first. Low power, high endurance. Minimal growth potential.

Type IIa (Fast Twitch)

Moderate threshold. Balanced power and endurance. Solid growth potential.

Type IIx (High Threshold)

Recruited under heavy load or near failure. Maximum growth potential.

The SRA Cycle

Stimulus, Recovery, Adaptation. Growth doesn't happen in the gym; it happens while you sleep. The workout is the signal; the recovery is the response.

1
Signal: Heavy Resistance Training
2
Repair: Protein Synthesis exceeds Breakdown
3
Grow: Myofibrillar cross-sectional area increases
